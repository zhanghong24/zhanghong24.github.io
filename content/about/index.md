{{< lead >}}
Hi, I'm Hong Zhang, a first-year PhD student passionate about High-Performance Computing (HPC) and High-Order Numerical Methods for fluid dynamics.

My research lies at the intersection of Physics and Computer Science. I am dedicated to developing next-generation CFD solvers that are not only mathematically rigorous but also computationally efficient on modern heterogeneous architectures (GPUs).

Currently, I am working on porting legacy Fortran WCNS solvers to modern C++/CUDA frameworks, exploring the potential of implicit algorithms (like LU-SGS) on large-scale multi-node GPU clusters (During 29 Dec 2025).

{{< /lead >}}
