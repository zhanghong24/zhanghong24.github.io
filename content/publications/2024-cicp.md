## Abstract

We introduce an efficient class of **Adaptive Stencil Extension (ASE)** reconstruction methods based on the Discontinuity Feedback (DF) factor. Unlike conventional WENO schemes that degrade from high to low order, our method **adaptively increases the order** from the lowest level based on local smoothness.

This approach **eliminates the need for computationally expensive smoothness indicators** (like $\beta_k$ in WENO), making it easily extensible to 9th-order or even higher.

---

## ğŸ’¡ Core Innovation: ASE-DF Strategy

The traditional WENO paradigm is "Start High $\to$ Drop Low".
**Our ASE-DF paradigm is "Start Low $\to$ Climb High".**

We use the DF factor to determine if we can safely extend the stencil:

$$
\mathbb{P}_{final}(x) = 
\begin{cases} 
\mathbb{P}^{r9}(x) & \text{if } \alpha_{\mathbb{S}^{r9}} = 1 \text{ (Very Smooth)} \\
\mathbb{P}^{r7}(x) & \text{if } \alpha_{\mathbb{S}^{r7}} = 1 \\
\text{WENO-AO}(5,3) & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
$$

This logic avoids calculating complex smoothness indicators for 7th and 9th orders, significantly reducing computational cost.
