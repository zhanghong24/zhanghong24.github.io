## Abstract

In this paper, a robustness-enhanced reconstruction for the high-order finite volume scheme is constructed on the 2-D structured mesh. To improve the robustness of the algorithm in discontinuous regions, a **hybrid reconstruction** based on a combination of **Discontinuity Feedback Factor (DF)** and WENO reconstruction is developed.

Numerical results, ranging from smooth flows to extreme cases (such as the hurricane-like problem with $Ma=16.0$), validate that the new scheme effectively enhances robustness while maintaining high resolution compared to the classical WENO scheme.

---

## ğŸš€ Key Method: Discontinuity Feedback (DF)

The core innovation is the **Discontinuity Feedback Factor (DF)**, which detects strong discontinuities based on pressure and Mach number variations at Gaussian points.

The DF factor $\alpha_{p,m}$ is defined as:

$$
\alpha_{p,m} = \frac{1}{1+D^2}
$$

where the discontinuity sensor $D$ combines pressure jumps and Mach number differences:

$$
D = \frac{|p^l - p^r|}{p^l} + \frac{|p^l - p^r|}{p^r} + (Ma_n^l - Ma_n^r)^2 + (Ma_t^l - Ma_t^r)^2
$$

### Hybrid Strategy
We introduced a threshold $\alpha_{thres}$ (typically 0.5). The scheme automatically switches to a robust 3rd-order polynomial when:

$$
\max\{\alpha_{i-1,j}, \alpha_{i,j}, \alpha_{i+1,j}\} < \alpha_{thres}
$$

This ensures that the scheme survives extreme shock conditions where standard WENO might fail.
